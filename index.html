The Problem

Trees are beautiful.
Their impact isn't.

Australian councils spend millions annually managing street trees that damage footpaths, crack kerbs, cause allergies, and require constant pruning — while simultaneously facing the looming challenge of curbside EV charging for streets with no off-street parking.

🪨

Root Damage

Tree roots lift footpaths, crack kerbs, and infiltrate stormwater pipes. Councils face ongoing repair costs and public liability claims from trip hazards — particularly dangerous for elderly residents and wheelchair users.

✂️

Pruning & Maintenance

Street trees require regular pruning around powerlines, buildings, and sightlines. Arborist costs run $300–$1,500 per tree per visit, multiplied across thousands of trees per municipality.

🤧

Allergens

London Plane Trees — Melbourne's most common street tree — produce aggressive pollen and fine seed hairs that trigger severe allergic reactions and respiratory issues for residents every spring and summer.

🔌

No EV Infrastructure

Thousands of inner-suburban streets have zero off-street parking. Residents will need curbside EV charging, but there's no infrastructure pathway to deliver it — especially where tree roots already occupy the nature strip.

💡

Aging Light Poles

Many street lights are decades old, inefficient, and due for replacement. Separate poles for lighting, signage, CCTV, and future 5G create visual clutter and redundant infrastructure.

🌡️

Urban Heat

Removing problem trees without replacement eliminates canopy cover, worsening the urban heat island effect in Melbourne summers that are becoming increasingly extreme.

The Solution

A power pole that looks like a tree.
A tree that works like infrastructure.

The Digital Tree is a modular aluminium pole with welded or clip-on branch arms that hold purpose-built pots of cascading, fast-growing plants. The structure disappears under living greenery while housing EV charging, LED lighting, CCTV, 5G, and more inside the trunk.

EV Charging

Inground inductive pads or pop-up bollard chargers powered through the pole. No cables across footpaths. Revenue stream for councils.

LED Street Lighting

Energy-efficient, remotely dimmable LED lighting integrated at the top of the pole, replacing aging standalone light posts.

CCTV & Security

Discreet camera housings within the branch structure for public safety monitoring without the visual intrusion of obvious camera poles.

5G / WiFi

Small cell 5G antennas and public WiFi access points concealed within the pole trunk, supporting smart city connectivity.

Environmental Sensors

Air quality, temperature, noise, and humidity monitoring — feeding real-time data to council dashboards for urban planning.

Automated Irrigation

Water supply runs through the hollow pole to drip lines in each branch arm. Timer-controlled with moisture sensors. Minimal maintenance.

Seasonal Canopy

Deciduous plants on upper branches provide summer shade and drop leaves for winter sun — mimicking natural tree behaviour.

Swappable Pots

Dead or overgrown plants replaced in minutes by a maintenance crew with a cherry picker. No arborist required. Pre-grown replacements ready to clip in.

Engineering

How it's built

Every component uses proven, off-the-shelf technology from established Australian suppliers. No R&D breakthroughs required — just smart integration of existing systems.

🏗️

Main Pole (Trunk)

Modular aluminium smart poles already exist and are deployed in streetscapes across Australia. Marine-grade 6000-series structural aluminium, extruded in Melbourne, with bolt-on modular sections. Lighter than steel, corrosion-proof, infinitely recyclable. Standard heights of 6–9m in stacking sections with pre-welded mounting collars for branch arms.

🌿

Branch Arms (Curved Aluminium)

CNC mandrel-bent aluminium tube (50–75mm diameter) with flange plates that bolt to mounting collars on the main pole. Think ring-lock scaffolding connections — branch arms pin or bolt in at predefined zone heights. Curved tube bending is standard fabrication work. Alternative: cast aluminium branch nodes using 3D-printed master patterns for organic Y-shaped forks.

🪴

Planter Troughs (Clip-On)

Custom fibreglass or GRC (glass-reinforced concrete) troughs shaped to cradle into the branch arms. Lightweight, UV-stable, with built-in drainage and sub-irrigation reservoirs. The clip-on mechanism is a simple U-bracket with a pin lock — cherry picker pulls the pin, lifts the old trough, drops in a pre-planted replacement. The 5-minute pot swap.

  • Sigma Planters / IOTA Australia ↗

💧

Drip Irrigation System

Water line runs up inside the hollow main pole from a base-mounted pump, tees off at each mounting collar into the hollow branch arms, and drips into each trough via standard 13mm poly fittings. This is identical to drip irrigation on a pergola or green wall — any irrigation supplier or plumber can install it. Smart controller with moisture sensors, timer, and optional rain gauge integration.

Standard irrigation components from any supplier — Reece, Bunnings, Holman, Pope, Netafim.

Est. cost: $500–$800 (pump, controller, lines, fittings)

⚡

Integrated Utilities

LED street lighting, CCTV, 5G small cells, WiFi access points, and EV charging are all existing bolt-on accessories for modular smart poles. Companies like Multipole and HUB already manufacture these accessory modules with standardised mounting systems. The Digital Tree simply adds the planting layer on top of proven smart city infrastructure.

Utility costs vary by council specification and are typically funded separately.

Crimson Glory Vine

Vitis coignetiae

An extremely vigorous deciduous vine with huge heart-shaped leaves up to 30cm across. Dense summer canopy of dark green foliage transforms into spectacular crimson, scarlet, and purple in autumn. Grows 2–5 metres per year. Always healthy — unlike fruiting grapes, it's disease-resistant and low-maintenance.

A vigorous non-fruiting hybrid widely grown in Australia, especially South Australia, since the early 1900s. Glossy leaves emerge coppery, mature to grey-green, then blaze amber, orange, and brilliant red in autumn. Thrives in Melbourne's climate — hot and dry to cool and moist.

Why This Works

Digital Tree vs. Traditional Street Tree

A side-by-side look at how the Digital Tree solves the problems that traditional street trees create — while keeping the beauty.

Factor Traditional Street Tree Digital Tree
Root Damage ✗ Ongoing — lifts footpaths, cracks kerbs, infiltrates pipes ✓ Zero — no roots in the ground
Pruning Costs ✗ $300–$1,500 per tree, per visit, arborist required ✓ Pot swap by general maintenance crew, 5 minutes
Allergens ✗ Plane Trees trigger severe pollen/hair allergies ✓ Species selected to be low-allergen
EV Charging ✗ Not possible — roots occupy underground space ✓ Built-in — inground charging from the pole
Street Lighting ✗ Separate pole required — visual clutter ✓ Integrated — LED lighting in the structure
CCTV / 5G / WiFi ✗ Additional poles and infrastructure needed ✓ All housed within the trunk
Summer Shade ✓ Dense canopy ✓ Deciduous climbers provide full canopy
Winter Sun ✓ Deciduous species allow light through ✓ Same — deciduous species on upper canopy
Beauty ✓ Established, loved by residents ✓ Flowering vines, seasonal colour, organic form
Carbon Sequestration ✓ Good — a mature tree captures ~22kg CO₂/year ✓ Comparable — fast-growing vines + dense planting
Community Value Passive — people enjoy shade and aesthetics ✓ Active — edible plants, herbs, engagement
Lifespan 50–100+ years (but with ongoing damage) Pole: 50+ years. Plants: refreshed continuously
Revenue Potential ✗ None ✓ EV charging fees, data/connectivity, advertising
Getting Started

From idea to prototype

The Digital Tree doesn't require inventing new technology. It requires combining existing components in a way nobody has done before.

01

Document the Problem

Photograph and map damaged footpaths, lifted kerbs, and pruning scars on streets in Elwood, Balaclava, and surrounding suburbs. Build the visual case for change.

02

Council Conversations

Informal chats with infrastructure contacts at Hobsons Bay, Port Phillip, and Bayside councils. Validate the pain point and understand procurement pathways.

03

Design & Render

Commission 3D renders of the Digital Tree concept — a modular smart pole with branch arms and cascading plants. Show it in an actual Melbourne streetscape context.

04

Small Prototype

Build a single branch section with pot brackets and planted climbers. Photograph it over one growing season to show how the structure disappears under greenery.

05

Partner & Apply

Partner with an existing smart pole manufacturer (Multipole, Urban Aluminium) and apply for Sustainability Victoria or ARENA grants for a full-scale prototype.

06

Pilot Street

Work with a council to install 3–5 Digital Trees on a single street. Monitor growth, maintenance costs, public response, and infrastructure performance over 12 months.
